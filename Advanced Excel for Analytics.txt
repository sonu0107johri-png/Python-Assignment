Q.2. What specific characteristics distinguish a "Key Performance Indicator" (KPI) from a standard business metric?

A.2. Key Performance Indicator (KPI) as the name suggest, is the key parameter that directly and influentially affects the outcome of data. While standard business metric may or may not be consequential for the entire understanding of the data and dataset. A KPI is a strategic metric tied directly to a business goal while a standard business metric is simply a measurement. Simply understanding, a business metric means any business activity like website visitors, employees on leaves, transport cost etc. These are useful measurements, but may not directly determine success. A KPI is important, goal-oriented and performance-focused. It directly shows whether the business is achieving an important objective or not like revenue, sales, cash flows, employee conditions etc. KPIs are the focus of the major decision making in the proper growth of the business, keeping other standard business metrics in mind.

Q.3. Classify Average Order Value (AOV) and Customer Repeat Rate as either Descriptive or Diagnostic KPIs and state the questions they answer.

A.3. A Descriptive KPI is a KPI that describes - What has already happened. It focuses on historic or current performance data. Purpose of the Descriptive KPI is to monitor performance, summarize business activity, understand trends, and track past outcomes. They do NOT predict the future directly. A Diagnostic KPI is a KPI used to understand - why did something happened. It helps analysts identify - causes, problems, root reasons, and performance drivers. Main purpose of diagnostic KPI is to tell "why did it happen" in contrast to "What happened." as in descriptive KPI. 
Based on the definitions of the descriptive and diagnostic KPIs, I would like to classify Average Order Value into the former category as it summarizes the customer purchasing behavior. Customer Repeat Rate automatically falls in the Diagnostic KPI as it determines how many customers return and purchase again.

Q.6. Why is treating raw data columns (like UnitPrice) as KPIs incorrect, and what transformation steps are required to create a meaningful KPI?  

A.6. Treating raw data columns like UnitPrice, Quantity, CustomerID, OrderDate as KPIs is incorrect because raw columns are individual data fields - NOT performance indicators. A KPI must:
	- measure performance
	- support decision-making
	- align with business goals
	- provide analytical meaning
By itself, UnitPrice = ₹10 tells almost nothing. Question remains:
	- Is ₹10 good or bad?
	- Compared to what.
	- Is revenue increasing?
	- Are profits healthy?
	- Are customers buying more?
So raw columns are only building blocks of analysis and not actual business indicators.
Transformation means - processing raw data into meaningful business measurements using calculations, aggregation, grouping, filtering, comparisons, ratios, and trends. Let us understand with an example. Raw columns are UnitPrice and Quantity with respective values as ₹100 for 5. Raw data alone is not KPI. But lets transform into Revenue as Revenue = UnitPrice x Quantity. Now you can get:
	- Total Sales
	- Monthly Revenue
	- Average Order Value
	- Profit Margin
These become meaningful KPIs.
Required Transformation Steps:
	1. Data Cleaning:
		- remove duplicates
		- handle missing values
		- fix datatype issues
	2. Aggregation:
		Combine raw records into summaries like daily revenue, monthly sales, customer totals etc.
	3. Calculation:
		Create derived metrics like Average Order Value = Revenue / Order or Profit Margin = (Profit / Revenue) x 100
	4. Business Context:
		Align with objective like revenue growth, customer retention, and operational efficiency
	5. Visualization and Trend Analysis:    
		Turn metrics into dashboards, trends, comparisons, and targets which are actionable KPs
